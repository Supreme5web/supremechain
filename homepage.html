Cryptocurrency Basics

Cryptocurrency is a digital or virtual form of currency that uses cryptography for security. Unlike traditional currencies issued by governments, cryptocurrencies operate on decentralized networks based on blockchain technology.

Key concepts include blockchain, wallets, private keys, and transactions. Understanding these fundamentals is essential for anyone entering the crypto space.

Crypto Trading

Crypto trading involves speculating on price movements via a CFD trading account, or buying and selling the underlying coins via an exchange.

Popular trading strategies include day trading, swing trading, and HODLing. Always remember that crypto markets are highly volatile and risky.

Decentralized Finance (DeFi)

DeFi is an emerging financial technology based on secure distributed ledgers similar to those used by cryptocurrencies.

DeFi eliminates the fees that banks and other financial companies charge for using their services. Key components include lending platforms, decentralized exchanges, and yield farming.

Non-Fungible Tokens (NFTs)

NFTs are cryptographic assets on a blockchain with unique identification codes and metadata that distinguish them from each other.

Unlike cryptocurrencies, they cannot be traded or exchanged at equivalency. This differs from fungible tokens like cryptocurrencies, which are identical to each other and can serve as a medium for commercial transactions.

Crypto Mining

Cryptocurrency mining is the process in which transactions between users are verified and added to the blockchain public ledger.

The process of mining is also responsible for introducing new coins into the existing circulating supply and is one of the key elements that allow cryptocurrencies to work as a peer-to-peer decentralized network.
